    Red Fort ,Delhi

    The Red Fort is one of the most significant monuments in the country. India's Prime Minister hoists the National Flag from the ramparts of the Red Fort each year on Independence

    Hawa Mahal, Jaipur

    Hawa Mahal is also called the palace of winds. Hawa Mahal is an emblem of the Rajputana architecture. In this Palace, there are 953 small windows called jharokhas that are decorated with intricate latticework. It is a five-story pyramidal shaped architecture.

    Visit Now ☛

    Chittorgarh Fort

    The Chittorgarh Fort also popularly known as Water Fort and is home to 22 water bodies, palaces, towers and temples spread over 700 acres. The fort was captured numerous times; the first one to lay his hands on the fort was Bappa Rawal of the Guhila dynasty. .

    Visit Now ☛

    Goa Beach

    Goa can be the perfect destination for a short holiday. Its sandy beaches and sunny weather attract people from across the globe. Moreover, it has multiple options for entertainment, like parties and nightlife, which also add to an exquisite holiday.

    Visit Now ☛

    Taj Mahal

    Taj Mahal is essentially a tomb built in the memory of Shah Jahan's wife, Mumtaz Mahal. Taj Mahal is also famous as the symbol of love. The popular dome and the pillars around the Taj Mahal are common in medieval Mughal architecture. .

    Visit Now ☛

    Adiyogi,Tamilnadu

    The Adiyogi statue is a 34-metre tall (112 ft), 45-metre long (147 ft) and 25-metre wide (82 ft) steel statue of Shiva with Thirunamam at Coimbatore, Tamil Nadu. It is recognized by the Guinness World Records as the "Largest Bust Sculpture” in the world.
